Technical note

How the Metro healthy life expectancy estimates were made

This is a calibrated synthetic small-area estimator for station-local healthy life expectancy. It is not an official ONS station estimate, and it should not be read as one. It is the methodological follow-on to the Metro life expectancy map.

Plain-English summary

A Bayesian model is a formal way of updating what is already known with new evidence. It starts with an initial expectation, called a prior, compares that with the local evidence, called the likelihood, and combines them into an updated estimate, called the posterior.

Bayesian borrowing means that a small-area estimate is allowed to learn from related information instead of relying only on a small local sample. Here, the related information is official local-authority HLE, the ONS deprivation gradient, MSOA life expectancy, and Census health and disability measures. The station area still matters, but it is not asked to carry more statistical weight than the available evidence can support.

Feasibility judgement

Current official healthy life expectancy at birth is available for larger local areas, but not for the MSOAs around every Metro station. A station map is therefore feasible only as a modelled estimate. The statistically defensible route is Bayesian borrowing: use official larger-area HLE as the anchor, use a local covariate model for station-area pattern, use the ONS deprivation gradient as a prior, and make the uncertainty and limitations visible.

Disability-free life expectancy was not used as the main outcome here. It remains a relevant health-expectancy measure, but this map needs a current deprivation benchmark and local HLE calibration target. HLE is therefore the clearer current basis for this offshoot; DFLE could be a separate sensitivity analysis if suitable local disability inputs and geography are assembled. For ONS health state life expectancy methods, see the ONS health state life expectancies QMI.

Data sources

Official HLE target OHID Fingertips indicator 90362, healthy life expectancy at birth, counties and unitary authorities, 2022 to 2024, male and female. Source: OHID Fingertips and ONS health state life expectancies.
Deprivation prior ONS healthy life expectancy by national area deprivation, England, 2022 to 2024. The map uses the published broad deprivation gradient as an external prior: 49.8 to 69.2 healthy years for males and 48.2 to 68.5 healthy years for females from the most to least deprived areas. Source: ONS HLE by national area deprivation.
MSOA mortality input OHID Fingertips indicator 93283, MSOA life expectancy at birth, 2019 to 2023, male and female.
Census predictors Nomis Census 2021 bulk tables: TS037 general health, TS038 disability, TS007 age by single year and TS008 sex. See Nomis Census 2021 bulk downloads.
Geography ONS MSOA 2021 to counties and unitary authorities 2023 best-fit lookup, and ONS MSOA 2021 boundary service for station point assignment. See the lookup dataset and MSOA boundary dataset.
Station points Department for Transport NaPTAN access nodes, using station-level records where StopType is MET. See NaPTAN data portal.

Estimator structure

The model has two layers. The first layer is a calibrated synthetic small-area model. It was fitted separately for males and females at county/unitary authority level. The target was official HLE at birth for 2022 to 2024. Predictors were population-weighted or population-aggregated area measures derived from MSOAs:

  • MSOA life expectancy at birth, aggregated with sex-specific population weights.
  • Proportion in very good or good self-rated health.
  • Proportion in bad or very bad self-rated health.
  • Proportion disabled under the Equality Act.
  • Proportion limited a lot in day-to-day activities.
  • Proportion with no long-term physical or mental health condition.
  • Age-structure proportions: 0 to 15, 65 plus and 85 plus.
  • Sex-specific population share.

For sex s and authority a, the first-stage fitted model is a weighted Gaussian regression. Let \(Y_{as}\) denote official authority HLE, \(\mathbf{x}_{as}\) the standardised predictor vector, and \(w_{as}\) the sex-specific Census population weight:

\[ Y_{as} = \alpha_s + \mathbf{x}_{as}^{\top}\boldsymbol{\beta}_s + \varepsilon_{as}, \qquad \varepsilon_{as} \sim N\!\left(0, \frac{\sigma_s^2}{w_{as}}\right). \]

Estimation uses the ridge objective

\[ (\hat{\alpha}_s,\hat{\boldsymbol{\beta}}_s) = \arg\min_{\alpha,\boldsymbol{\beta}} \left[ \sum_a w_{as}\{Y_{as}-\alpha-\mathbf{x}_{as}^{\top}\boldsymbol{\beta}\}^2 + \lambda_s \sum_j \beta_j^2 \right]. \]

In Bayesian terms this is a normal prior on the regression coefficients, \(\beta_{js}\sim N(0,\tau_s^2)\), with the intercept unpenalised. The shrinkage is used because the authority-level training set is modest and the health, disability, age and mortality predictors are correlated. Predictors were standardised before fitting. The ridge penalty was selected by deterministic 10-fold cross-validation from a fixed grid. The selected penalty was \(\lambda_s=10.0\) for males and \(\lambda_s=1.0\) for females.

The second layer is the map-facing Bayesian borrowing step. For each mapped station \(i\), the calibrated local covariate estimate is treated as a noisy likelihood and the ONS deprivation-gradient estimate is treated as a prior. If \(D_i\in\{1,\ldots,10\}\) is the station-area IMD decile, the ONS deprivation benchmark for sex \(s\) is linearly interpolated as

\[ p_{is}=d_{1s} + \frac{D_i-1}{9}(d_{10s}-d_{1s}), \]

where \((d_{1s}, d_{10s})\) are the ONS HLE values for the most and least deprived English areas. This prior is centred within local authority before borrowing:

\[ \mu^{(p)}_{is}=Y_{a(i)s}+p_{is}-\bar{p}_{a(i)s}. \]

With \(z_{is}\) denoting the first-stage calibrated station estimate and \(SE^{(1)}_{is}\) its standard error, the borrowing model is

\[ z_{is}\mid\theta_{is}\sim N\!\left(\theta_{is}, \{SE^{(1)}_{is}\}^2\right), \qquad \theta_{is}\sim N\!\left(\mu^{(p)}_{is}, \sigma_p^2\right), \qquad \sigma_p=1.0. \]

Because this is a conjugate normal-normal model, the posterior mean and variance are available in closed form:

\[ \hat{\theta}_{is} = \frac{z_{is}/\{SE^{(1)}_{is}\}^2 + \mu^{(p)}_{is}/\sigma_p^2} {1/\{SE^{(1)}_{is}\}^2 + 1/\sigma_p^2}, \qquad \{SE^{(post)}_{is}\}^2 = \left(1/\{SE^{(1)}_{is}\}^2 + 1/\sigma_p^2\right)^{-1}. \]

A simulation-based MCMC fit would give the same posterior mean for this Gaussian borrowing step. The closed-form update is simpler, transparent and reproducible for a draft map.

Calibration step

The first-stage model was applied to each MSOA to produce a raw synthetic HLE prediction. Within each authority and sex, the raw MSOA predictions were shifted so their sex-population-weighted mean exactly matched the official authority HLE:

\[ z_{ms} = Y_{a(m)s} + \tilde{\theta}_{ms} - \bar{\tilde{\theta}}_{a(m)s}, \qquad \bar{\tilde{\theta}}_{as} = \frac{\sum_{m\in a} n_{ms}\tilde{\theta}_{ms}} {\sum_{m\in a} n_{ms}}. \]

For the map display, the posterior station estimates are also centred within each authority for the mapped station set. This keeps the map visibly anchored to the published authority HLE while allowing the station pattern to borrow from deprivation and local covariates. It does not make the within-authority pattern official; that part is still modelled.

\[ \theta^{(map)}_{is} = Y_{a(i)s} + \hat{\theta}_{is} - \bar{\hat{\theta}}_{a(i)s}. \]

This centring is a conservative choice for a public-facing map: it prevents the display from drifting away from published local HLE, but it also means the mapped values are not independent direct observations.

Uncertainty intervals

The station intervals are model intervals, not official confidence intervals. The first-stage standard error combines four components:

  • Authority-level cross-validated model error.
  • Published official authority HLE standard error, approximated from the 95% confidence interval.
  • MSOA life-expectancy input standard error, scaled by the fitted life-expectancy coefficient.
  • An extra imputation penalty where an MSOA life-expectancy input was suppressed and replaced by the authority-sex mean.

The map-facing interval then uses the posterior standard error from the normal-normal borrowing step plus a 2.0-year structural uncertainty floor. That floor is included because the station-level HLE target is unobserved. The interval is intentionally conservative enough for public interpretation, but it still cannot capture every source of small-area model uncertainty.

\[ SE^{(1)}_{is} = \sqrt{ RMSE_s^2 + SE(Y_{a(i)s})^2 + \{\hat{\beta}_{LE,s}SE(LE_{is})\}^2 + I_{is}RMSE_s^2 }, \] \[ SE^{(map)}_{is} = \sqrt{\{SE^{(post)}_{is}\}^2+\sigma_{struct}^2}, \qquad \sigma_{struct}=2.0, \] \[ 95\% \text{ model interval} = \theta^{(map)}_{is}\pm 1.96\,SE^{(map)}_{is}. \]

Diagnostics from this run

  • 60 Metro station records were matched to MSOAs; none were unmatched in the source model.
  • 59 stations are shown on the map, matching the original life-expectancy Metro artwork. Manors is excluded from the map because the original map reference dropped it.
  • 151 authorities per sex were used for model training after missing official HLE targets were excluded.
  • Cross-validated RMSE was 1.50 years for males and 1.72 years for females.
  • Cross-validated MAE was 1.15 years for males and 1.35 years for females.
  • The maximum post-calibration authority error was less than 0.000001 years.
  • One station-sex input required life-expectancy imputation: male life expectancy for Manors.
  • The map-facing Bayesian-borrowed male range is 49.5 to 69.3 healthy years.
  • The map-facing Bayesian-borrowed female range is 49.0 to 70.1 healthy years.
  • The broad map-facing gaps are 19.8 years for males and 21.1 years for females.

Important limitations

  • These are not official ONS HLE estimates for stations or MSOAs.
  • The model is ecological. It estimates area averages, not individual healthy life expectancy.
  • A station value refers to the residential MSOA containing the station point, not to Metro passengers or people born at the station.
  • The within-authority station pattern is modelled from covariates; it is not directly validated against current observed MSOA HLE, because current observed MSOA HLE is not published.
  • Calibration ensures official authority averages are reproduced, but it does not prove that the model has the correct within-authority spread.
  • The ONS deprivation prior makes the map more plausible against published deprivation HLE, but it also means the mapped estimates are partly prior-driven.
  • The 1.0-year deprivation prior standard deviation and 2.0-year structural uncertainty floor are modelling judgements. A final publication should include sensitivity checks.
  • The map-facing authority centring is applied to the mapped station set, not to a population-weighted sample of all neighbourhoods in the authority.
  • Census health and disability predictors are from Census Day, 21 March 2021. The mortality input covers 2019 to 2023, and official HLE covers 2022 to 2024.
  • The 2019 to 2023 MSOA life-expectancy input includes the COVID-19 pandemic period.
  • Census small-area counts are affected by disclosure-control methods including record swapping and cell key perturbation.
  • The MSOA general-health and disability predictors used here are raw counts and rates. Age structure is included in the model, but these are not directly observed age-sex-specific Sullivan inputs.
  • Sex-specific HLE is modelled using sex-specific mortality and population structure, but the MSOA self-rated health and disability predictors are not sex-specific in the bulk tables used here.
  • Nearby stations can share an MSOA and therefore share the same estimate.
  • Manors is available in the station-estimate file but is not shown on this map, so the HLE follow-on visually matches the original life-expectancy post.
  • Station assignment can be sensitive to boundary placement for stations near an MSOA edge.
  • The uncertainty intervals do not include every possible source of model uncertainty, source-data revision or geography change.
  • The model shows association and spatial pattern. It should not be interpreted as a causal estimate of transport access, deprivation or any single policy lever.
  • ONS describes current health state life expectancies as official statistics in development, so future methodological changes could revise the official HLE target used for calibration.

Reproducibility files

The pipeline is implemented in scripts/build-metro-hle-model.py. Running it writes:

  • stories/metro-healthy-life-expectancy/data/station-hle-estimates.csv
  • stories/metro-healthy-life-expectancy/data/station-hle-estimates.json
  • stories/metro-healthy-life-expectancy/data/station-hle-map-data.json
  • stories/metro-healthy-life-expectancy/data/model-diagnostics.json
