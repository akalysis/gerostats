Why this page

A practical front door to a rich cohort

The Newcastle 85+ Study began in 2006 and recruited more than 1,000 people born in 1921 from Newcastle and North Tyneside. It combines multidimensional health assessments, general-practice record review, function tests, biological measures and long-term follow-up.

This guide does not replace the official Newcastle University study website. It gives prospective data users a shorter route through it: what to read, where to find measures, what to put in an application and who to contact when something is unclear.

04 · Prepare

Make the first conversation useful

You do not need a finished protocol. You do need enough detail to test whether the idea is answerable with this cohort.

Bring a one-page project sketch

  • Question: one primary question or hypothesis.
  • Rationale: three sentences on the gap and why Newcastle 85+ is needed.
  • Sample: phases, inclusion criteria and expected follow-up.
  • Variables: exposure, outcome, confounders and their source schedules.
  • Analysis: the proposed model and how death, dropout and missingness will be handled.
  • Output: intended paper, report or other product, with a realistic timetable.

Before you submit

Use the study channel for current requirements

Data access is governed by the Newcastle 85+ Study, and charges may apply. Confirm the current process before collecting signatures or making firm project plans.
