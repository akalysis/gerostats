Gerostats

I am Andrew Kingston. Gerostats is where I put the numbers that stay with me: ageing, care, disability, deprivation and the local geographies that make inequality feel close rather than abstract. I think of it as a digital garden for health statistics: planted notes, live methods, data stories and tools that can keep growing.

Enter the garden About Gerostats

What this is

A place to work through ageing, health and inequality.

Gerostats is for the questions that keep coming back in my work and in my own sense of place: who grows older in good health, who provides care, where disability clusters, and why some geographies make inequality feel almost ordinary. The pieces here are part data story, part method note, part field notebook.

Ideas that can be revised, replanted and grown as the evidence changes.
