Gerostats

I'm Andrew Kingston, a Geordie who grew up around West Denton and Newbiggin Hall in the West End of Newcastle. Gerostats is my digital garden for health statistics, ageing and later life: a personal place to collect notes, data stories, methods, visualisations and unfinished thoughts about the numbers that shape how we age, how we live, and how inequality becomes visible.

What this is

A working notebook for questions that keep coming back.

I use Gerostats to work through the health-statistics questions I keep coming back to: ageing, care, disability, place and inequality. Some pieces are finished. Some are notes to myself in public. Some are data musings that are still finding their shape.

The data stories often begin personally and locally, because familiar places make public numbers harder to treat as abstract. The questions travel further than that: ageing, disability, care need and health inequality are local experiences, but they are also international research problems.

Author

Andrew Kingston

I am a statistical epidemiologist from the North East and a Senior Lecturer at Newcastle University. I work on ageing, multimorbidity, disability, care need, health expectancy and health inequalities. The site keeps that work grounded: many of the stories start close to home, but the questions do not stop at the edge of the map.
