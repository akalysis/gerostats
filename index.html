Gerostats

Gerostats is a digital garden for health statistics, ageing and later life. It is where I collect notes, data stories, methods, visualisations and unfinished thoughts about the numbers that shape how we age, how we live, and how inequality becomes visible.

What this is

What is Gerostats?

Gerostats sits somewhere between a blog, a notebook and a public methods lab. Some pieces are polished essays. Some are working notes. Some are explanations of statistical ideas. Some are reflections on data, place, policy and ageing. The point is to make the thinking visible while it is still growing.

Core themes

Ageing, statistics, public health and later life.

Ageing and later life

The shape of later life is changing. Gerostats looks at how long people live, how well they live, and how care, disability and health are distributed across populations.

Disability and care

Disability is not random. Neither is care need. These patterns reflect lives, places, services, inequalities and policy choices.

Health inequality

Gerostats is interested in the gap between statistical averages and lived experience: who benefits, who is missed, and who carries the burden.

Statistical methods

Methods matter because they shape what becomes visible. Gerostats includes notes on modelling, uncertainty, causal thinking, simulation, health expectancy and population data.

Place

Health and ageing happen somewhere. Place matters: streets, homes, regions, local authorities, services, transport and the social geography of later life.

Public health futures

Gerostats is also about projection and imagination: what the future of later life might look like, and what choices could change it.
